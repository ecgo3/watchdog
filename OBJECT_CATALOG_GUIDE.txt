This file explains the groups shown in WATCHDOG's OBJECT CATALOG pane.
The pane is an index of public space-object records, not a list of objects
owned or operated by WATCHDOG.

The satellite catalog and group memberships come from CelesTrak. WATCHDOG
matches those public group records to the active CelesTrak SATCAT catalog and
displays the resulting count beside each category.

An object can belong to several categories at once. For example, a CubeSat can
also be an amateur-radio satellite, an Earth-observation satellite, and a
science mission. Category totals therefore must not be added together to find
the total number of unique satellites.

The groups describe what an object is or what it generally does. They do not
replace the individual object's data card.

AMATEUR RADIO

Satellites that carry equipment associated with the amateur-radio service.
They may transmit beacons or telemetry, relay voice or digital messages, or
provide experimental radio links for licensed amateur operators, universities,
and educational groups.
source group: CelesTrak "amateur"


BEIDOU

BeiDou is China's global navigation satellite system. Its satellites broadcast
timing and navigation signals that compatible receivers use to calculate
position, velocity, and time. It serves a role comparable to GPS, Galileo, and
GLONASS.

source group: CelesTrak "beidou"


COMMUNICATIONS

Satellites whose main public function is relaying information. This broad group
can include television and radio broadcasting, telephone links, internet
service, data relay, mobile communications, and government communications.

Dedicated constellations such as Starlink, OneWeb, and Kuiper are displayed as
their own groups even though their mission is also communications. A satellite
can consequently appear in both a constellation-specific group and a general
communications group.

ource group: CelesTrak "other-comm"


CUBESATS

CubeSat describes a standardized spacecraft size and construction format, not
a mission. One CubeSat unit, written 1U, is approximately a 10-centimeter cube.
Larger spacecraft combine units into formats such as 2U, 3U, 6U, or 12U.

source group: CelesTrak "cubesat"


EARTH OBS

Earth-observation satellites look toward Earth and collect remote-sensing data.
Payloads can include optical cameras, infrared instruments, radar, microwave
sensors, altimeters, and atmospheric instruments. Their measurements support
mapping, environmental research, agriculture, disaster response, climate
science, oceanography, and resource monitoring.


 source group: CelesTrak "resource"


GALILEO

Galileo is the European Union's global navigation satellite system. It provides
positioning, navigation, and precise timing services through a constellation
primarily operating in medium Earth orbit. It is a separate system from the
United States' GPS, Russia's GLONASS, and China's BeiDou.

source group: CelesTrak "galileo"


GLONASS

GLONASS is Russia's global navigation satellite system. Its satellites provide
positioning, navigation, and timing signals, primarily from medium Earth orbit.
Receivers may combine GLONASS with other navigation constellations to improve
coverage and measurement geometry.

source group: CelesTrak "glo-ops"


GPS

GPS is the United States' Global Positioning System. GPS satellites broadcast
precise time and orbital information from medium Earth orbit. A receiver uses
signals from multiple satellites to determine position, velocity, and time.

source group: CelesTrak "gps-ops"


KUIPER

Project Kuiper is Amazon's low-Earth-orbit broadband satellite constellation.
It is intended to provide internet connectivity through a large network of
satellites and user ground terminals.

 source group: CelesTrak "kuiper"


MILITARY PUBLIC

Military or defense-associated spacecraft that are present in public orbital
catalogs. Public records can provide a catalog identity and general orbital
elements, but detailed payload capabilities, telemetry, operators, maneuvers,
and mission objectives may be limited, generalized, delayed, or unavailable.

source group: CelesTrak "military"


ONEWEB

OneWeb is a low-Earth-orbit broadband communications constellation operated by
Eutelsat OneWeb. The satellites work as a network to connect user regions and
gateway stations. They occupy multiple orbital planes rather than functioning
as a single spacecraft.

source group: CelesTrak "oneweb"


SCIENCE

Spacecraft publicly grouped around scientific investigation. Missions can study
astronomy, solar physics, Earth's magnetosphere, space weather, geodesy,
fundamental physics, planetary environments, or the space environment itself.

This is a broad catalog group, not a complete list of all scientific missions.
Some science spacecraft are instead or additionally classified as CubeSats,
Earth-observation satellites, weather satellites, or deep-space spacecraft.

source group: CelesTrak "science"

STARLINK

Starlink is SpaceX's large low-Earth-orbit communications constellation for
broadband internet service. Thousands of individual satellites operate across
multiple orbital shells and planes. Each public catalog object has its own
NORAD ID and orbital element set even though it belongs to the wider network.

source group: CelesTrak "starlink"


WEATHER

Satellites used for meteorology and environmental monitoring. Their instruments
observe clouds, storms, atmospheric temperature and moisture, winds, radiation,
and other conditions. Some remain over one longitude in geostationary orbit;
others repeatedly scan the planet from lower polar or near-polar orbits.

source group: CelesTrak "weather"


OBJECT TAGS

[SAT]  Artificial Earth satellite.
[ISS]  Crewed station or human-spaceflight object classification.
[AST]  Asteroid or near-Earth object.
[COM]  Comet.
[PLN]  Planet or major Solar System body.
[SCI]  Scientific or deep-space spacecraft.
[---]  Object type was not identified by the source response.

An unknown tag does not mean the object is imaginary or invalid. It means the
available lookup result did not provide a type WATCHDOG could identify without
guessing.


COMMON DATA TERMS


NORAD ID
    The public catalog number assigned to a tracked Earth-orbiting object. It
    identifies a catalog object; it is not a radio frequency or mission number.

GP / TLE
    Public General Perturbations orbital elements. TLE means Two-Line Element
    set. WATCHDOG obtains current GP data from CelesTrak and propagates it with
    SGP4 to calculate a predicted state.

SGP4
    The standard mathematical propagation model used with public GP/TLE data.
    Its output is a prediction based on the element epoch, not live spacecraft
    telemetry.

ALTITUDE
    Approximate height above the reference Earth surface at the prediction time.

INCLINATION
    The angle between an orbit's plane and Earth's equatorial plane.

PERIGEE / APOGEE
    The lowest and highest portions of an Earth orbit, respectively.

OPERATOR
    Organization responsible for controlling a spacecraft. If the selected
    authoritative source does not publish it, WATCHDOG displays NOT PUBLIC or
    UNAVAILABLE rather than inferring it from the spacecraft name.

STATUS
    The operational code or state published by the source. Public status can lag
    real operations and should be read together with its source label.

PREDICTED POSITION
    A position calculated for a specific time from an authoritative public
    ephemeris or from public orbital elements and the labeled propagation model.
    It is not necessarily a live measurement.


HOW TO USE THE CATALOG

1. Press Tab until OBJECT CATALOG is active.
2. Move with j/k or the arrow keys.
3. Press Enter to load objects from the selected category.
4. Select an object in the OBJECTS pane and press Enter to inspect it.
5. Press Ctrl+V to open authoritative trajectory geometry in the external
   visualizer when geometry is available.

Equivalent commands include:

    satellite starlink
    satellite gps
    list cubesats
    list science
    search ISS
    inspect 25544


IMPORTANT LIMITS

- Public category membership is maintained by the external catalog producer
  and may change as objects are launched, reclassified, or decay.
- Category membership is not proof of a spacecraft's complete mission or
  current operational state.
- Public GP/TLE data is suitable for visualization and general prediction, not
  flight safety, conjunction assessment, targeting, or spacecraft control.
- Classified, withheld, or unpublished objects and telemetry are not available.
- WATCHDOG labels absent information instead of filling gaps with assumptions.
